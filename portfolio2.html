DATA, INFORMATION, AND INFORMATION SYSTEMS

DATA IS...

Raw, unrefined information that consists of basic facts, observations, or measurements. It is unprocessed and lacks context, making it meaningless on its own until it is analyzed or interpreted. Data can represent various elements like numbers, words, or sensory input, but by itself, it doesn't provide insights or understanding.

INFORMATION IS...

What we get when data is organized and connected in a meaningful way. It’s the result of linking different pieces of data to create understanding, helping us make sense of the world and define things more clearly. Information adds context to data, making it useful and relevant.

INFORMATION SYSTEM

Information System (IS) is a field that manages the collection, processing, and distribution of information for specific purposes. It involves designing, using, and maintaining systems that help businesses improve products and make informed decisions. IS is crucial for organizational success, with its effectiveness measured by factors like system quality and user satisfaction. In today’s world, ignoring IS can harm a business, as technology is vital to all aspects of management and growth.

TYPES OF INFORMATION SYSTEM
TPS

Transaction Processing System

The Transaction Processing System (TPS) manages real-world interactions between an enterprise and individuals or other businesses, facilitating exchanges such as information, products, services, or money. This system typically operates through computers, using internet communication and connected networks to carry out transactions efficiently.

MIS

Management Information System

The Management Information System (MIS) manages the flow of information within a business or organization. It ensures that information is directed to the appropriate systems, enabling efficient and effective communication. MIS plays a crucial role in business decision-making and supports various management functions, helping the organization operate smoothly and make informed choices.

DSS

Decision Support System

The Decision Support System (DSS) is a tool in the Information System field designed to assist and improve managerial decision-making. As technology advances, the data companies need to understand has expanded beyond traditional data, such as "Big Data." DSS plays a key role in managing this vast information, as it effectively supports decision-making by bridging the gap between managers and computers, making the decision process more efficient and informed.

EIS

Executive Information System

The Executive Information System (EIS) helps executives make faster, better decisions in a competitive, globalized environment. Case studies show EIS improves organizational intelligence, decision-making, and business understanding, leading to more comprehensive analysis, greater confidence, and quicker, higher-quality decisions, even in reduced staffing situations.
